Fiona the Hippo

By Mia Zanzucchi

<p>Out of the ashes of the Cincinnati Zoo’s Harambee (#RIP) rose the Cincinnati Zoo’s Fiona the Hippo, the round blob of grey that the internet needed in a 2017 full of dumpster-fire chaos.</p>

<p>Fiona the Nile Hippopotamus <a href="http://mashable.com/2017/11/12/fiona-the-hippo-best-moments/#vdQINIjjbqqn">was born</a> six weeks prematurely on Jan. 24, and the Cincinnati Zoo immediately began to document her care on social media. At just 29 pounds (less than half the weight of the average baby hippo), Fiona was far from living a normal hippo life, but the Cincinnati Zoo and the online world settled in for the long haul as staff and <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2017/11/25/style/fiona-the-hippo.html">even nurses</a> from the Cincinnati Children’s Hospital cared for her around the clock for the first few months of her life.</p><p><center><iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/yrn6A-VvETU" frameborder="0" gesture="media" allow="encrypted-media" allowfullscreen></iframe></center></p><p>What the darling grey lump lacked in teeth, she made up for in heart (and perseverance and cuteness). It was that lack of teeth that made her <a href="http://www.dailymail.co.uk/video/news/video-1462359/Teething-baby-hippo-Fiona-enjoys-check-mouth-massage.html">signature hippo pose</a> (mouth wide open, either trying to scare or waiting for a snack) so endearing and delightful. Her earlier days were spent competing with April the Giraffe, the disappointing Animal Adventure Park giraffe whose birth <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dz_errIBq3Y">livestream</a> on YouTube <a href="https://www.buzzfeed.com/juliareinstein/april-tweets?utm_term=.saOrW5XXx#.inqbVoWWR">took forever</a> and a day, keeping many waiting and watching as she did normal giraffe things like walk around. But in the end, America’s darling won out. Not surprising, since her cute little eyes, adorable teensy ears and winning smile break approximately 17 hearts an hour. </p><p><center><blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-lang="en"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">What are bubbles?Fiona isn’t sure!Caring for our animals includes providing them w/enrichment &amp; introducing them to new stimuli like bubbles <a href="https://t.co/FGX0WJcLaL">pic.twitter.com/FGX0WJcLaL</a></p>&mdash; Cincinnati Zoo (@CincinnatiZoo) <a href="https://twitter.com/CincinnatiZoo/status/868523098064257024?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">May 27, 2017</a></blockquote>
